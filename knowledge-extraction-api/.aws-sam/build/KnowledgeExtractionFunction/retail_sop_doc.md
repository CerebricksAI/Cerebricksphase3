## 1. Opening Procedures

### Pre-Opening Checklist (30 minutes before store opening)

**Manager Responsibilities:**
1. **Cash Register Setup** (15 minutes before opening)
   - Count opening cash drawer: $500 in mixed denominations
   - Verify breakdown: 20x $1, 10x $5, 8x $10, 2x $20, 1x $100
   - Complete Cash Count Form #CR-001
   - Sign and timestamp the form
   - Place form in safe drop box

2. **System Activation**
   - Boot all POS terminals (minimum 10 minutes startup time)
   - Verify internet connectivity on all stations
   - Test barcode scanners on each terminal
   - Print test receipt to confirm printer functionality
   - Check credit card reader response time (<3 seconds)

3. **Store Walk-Through** (Must be completed 10 minutes before opening)
   - Inspect all emergency exits (must be unobstructed)
   - Check restroom cleanliness and supplies
   - Verify all price tags match the POS system (random sample of 10 items)
   - Ensure promotional signage matches current weekly ad
   - Check floor for spills or safety hazards
   - Verify shopping carts/baskets are available and clean (minimum 20 carts)

4. **Team Brief** (5 minutes before opening)
   - Review daily sales goals
   - Announce any special promotions or events
   - Assign break schedules
   - Address any overnight communications
   - Remind team of current loss prevention focus items

**Opening Time:**
- Unlock front doors exactly at scheduled time (9:00 AM weekdays, 10:00 AM Sundays)
- Greet first customers with "Welcome to RetailCo!"
- Activate door counter system

## 2. Customer Returns and Exchanges

### General Return Policy
- **Time Frame:** 30 days from purchase with receipt
- **Extended Holiday Returns:** Items purchased Nov 1 - Dec 24 can be returned until Jan 15
- **Condition:** Items must be unused, with original tags and packaging

### Return Authorization Process

**Step 1: Verification**
- Request original receipt or order confirmation
- If no receipt: 
  - Lookup by credit card (swipe original payment card)
  - Lookup by phone number in loyalty program
  - Maximum 2 no-receipt returns per customer per year

**Step 2: Inspection**
- Verify item condition matches "like new" standard
- Check for signs of use, damage, or washing (apparel)
- Ensure all original tags are attached
- Confirm packaging is intact for electronics and sealed goods

**Step 3: Refund Processing**

| Original Payment | Refund Method | Processing Time |
|-----------------|---------------|-----------------|
| Credit Card | Same card | 3-5 business days |
| Cash | Cash | Immediate |
| Store Credit | Store Credit | Immediate |
| Gift Card | Gift Card | Immediate |

**Special Categories:**

**Electronics:**
- 14-day return window (not 30)
- Must include all original accessories, cables, manuals
- Restocking fee: 15% if opened
- Manager approval required for returns >$200

**Final Sale Items:**
- Clearance items marked with RED tag are final sale
- Holiday seasonal items after December 26
- Personalized or customized items
- Undergarments and swimwear (hygiene policy)

**Defective Items:**
- No time limit on manufacturer defects
- Exchange for same item or store credit
- Complete Defect Report Form #DR-500
- Take photos of defect before processing
- Send form to Quality Assurance team

### Fraud Red Flags
Watch for these signs and call manager immediately:
- Customer attempts multiple high-value returns in one day
- Receipt appears altered or photocopied
- Customer is aggressive or rushes the transaction
- Item shows signs of wear but customer claims it's unused
- Serial numbers don't match receipt
- Gift receipts for expensive electronics

**When to Deny a Return:**
- Item is outside return window
- Item shows clear signs of use
- No proof of purchase and item not in system
- Item is on the final sale list
- Suspected fraud (manager decision)

## 3. Inventory Management

### Daily Inventory Tasks

**Morning Stock Check (First hour of operation):**
1. Review overnight delivery manifests
2. Count high-value items (jewelry, electronics) against daily log
3. Check for stock discrepancies from previous day
4. Update "Out of Stock" board for customer service team

**Receiving Shipments:**

**Delivery Acceptance Checklist:**
- Verify driver credentials and delivery manifest
- Count boxes against manifest (sign only after count matches)
- Note any damaged boxes on manifest before signing
- Refuse delivery if more than 10% of boxes are damaged
- Take photos of any damage before opening

**Processing Received Goods (Same day as delivery):**
1. Open boxes in dedicated receiving area
2. Scan each item barcode into inventory system
3. Check actual items against packing slip
4. Flag discrepancies:
   - Missing items → File claim within 24 hours (Form #INV-DISC)
   - Damaged items → Photograph and file damage claim
   - Wrong items → Call distribution center immediately
5. Apply security tags to applicable items (see Loss Prevention section)
6. Stage items by department on receiving shelves
7. Stock items on sales floor within 4 hours of processing

**Cycle Counts:**
- High-theft items: Daily count
- Department feature items: Weekly count (every Monday)
- Full store inventory: Quarterly (March, June, Sept, Dec)

**Low Stock Alerts:**
- POS system auto-generates reorder alerts at 20% stock level
- Manager reviews and approves reorders daily
- Priority items (top 50 sellers) reordered immediately
- Seasonal items: increase par levels 2 weeks before season

### Loss Prevention Integration
- All inventory movements require POS system documentation
- No items leave the store without receipt or Transfer Form #TRF-100
- Employee packages must be checked by manager before leaving
- Trash removal requires manager inspection (check for concealed items)

## 4. Point of Sale Operations

### Transaction Best Practices

**Every Transaction Must Include:**
1. **Greeting:** "Hi! Did you find everything okay today?"
2. **Scan Verification:** Show customer the price on screen as you scan
3. **Promotion Check:** "You saved $X.XX today!" (if applicable)
4. **Payment Processing:** 
   - For credit: "Tap, insert chip, or swipe when ready"
   - For cash: Count back change to customer
5. **Loyalty Program:** 
   - If customer has account: "You've earned 50 points today!"
   - If no account: "Would you like to join our rewards program?"
6. **Receipt Offer:** "Would you like your receipt printed or emailed?"
7. **Closing:** "Thank you for shopping at RetailCo! Have a great day!"

**Transaction Time Targets:**
- Standard transaction: <3 minutes
- During peak hours: <2 minutes average
- Complex transactions (returns, multiple coupons): <5 minutes

### Handling Common POS Issues

**Barcode Won't Scan:**
1. Type SKU manually (found under barcode)
2. If SKU missing: Look up item by description in system
3. If item not found: Call manager for price verification
4. NEVER guess at price

**Price Discrepancy:**
- Customer says shelf price was different
- Check shelf tag with portable scanner
- If price is wrong on shelf:
  - Honor lower price (manager discretion if >$20 difference)
  - Radio stockroom to fix shelf tag immediately
  - Log on Price Correction Sheet
- If customer is mistaken: Politely show shelf tag photo

**Payment Declined:**
- Say: "I'm sorry, this payment method isn't going through"
- Offer to try again or use alternative payment
- NEVER say: "Your card is declined" (privacy concern)
- If customer becomes upset: Call manager immediately

**System Crash:**
1. Apologize to customers in line
2. Attempt POS reboot (hold power for 10 seconds)
3. If multiple terminals down: Call IT helpdesk (1-800-RETAIL-IT)
4. If network down: Use offline mode backup terminal
5. Manager makes decision: hold customers or offer rain check

### End of Shift Closeout

**Cash Drawer Reconciliation:**
1. Count all cash in drawer
2. Complete Cash Count Form #CR-001
3. Compare to POS system expected amount
4. Calculate variance (+/- $5 is acceptable)
5. If variance >$5: Manager must investigate before closeout
6. Place cash in sealed deposit bag with form
7. Drop bag in safe with two-person verification

**Credit Card Batch Close:**
- System auto-batches at 11:59 PM
- Print batch report for manager review
- File report in daily paperwork folder

## 5. Visual Merchandising Standards

### Display Guidelines

**Window Displays:**
- Refresh every 2 weeks
- Feature seasonal and promotional items
- Change after 5 PM (no customer view of setup)
- Use approved signage templates only
- Maximum 5 items per window to avoid clutter

**Feature Tables (Front of Store):**
- High-turnover items or current promotions
- Restock minimum 3x per day (open, lunch, evening)
- Keep display neat ("faced" and straightened)
- Price signs must be clearly visible from 10 feet away

**Endcaps (End of Aisles):**
- Reserved for weekly promotional items
- Set Sunday evening for Monday promotion start
- Never leave endcaps empty
- Use approved planogram from corporate

**Shelf Standards:**
- Products "faced" (front label out) at all times
- No gaps on shelves (fill with backup stock or shift items)
- Price tags aligned below product
- Dust shelves weekly (Saturday closing shift)

**Mannequins:**
- Dress in current season styles
- Complete outfit changes every 10 days
- Mix and match to show styling options
- Tag all items with SKU tags

### Signage Rules
- Only corporate-approved signage permitted
- No handwritten signs except temporary stock notes
- Sale signs: RED background with white text
- New arrivals: YELLOW tags
- Clearance: RED tags with slash-through price
- Remove expired promotional signs within 24 hours of promotion end

## 6. Loss Prevention Protocol

### High-Risk Items (Require Enhanced Security)

**Category A (Must have security tag):**
- Electronics over $50
- Designer handbags and accessories
- Jewelry (all items)
- Premium cosmetics
- Small high-value items (watches, sunglasses)

**Category B (Keep in locked case):**
- Items over $200
- Fragrance sets
- Professional tools
- Collectibles

**Security Tag Application:**
- Apply tag in receiving before floor display
- Place tag on seam or interior location (not easily removable)
- For apparel: Thread tag through seam, not just on hangtag
- Test tag activation at door sensor before stocking

### Shoplifting Prevention

**Active Customer Service:**
- Greet every customer within 30 seconds of entry
- Make eye contact and smile
- Offer assistance: "Let me know if you need any help!"
- Circle back to customers every 5 minutes in department

**Suspicious Behavior Indicators:**
- Large bags or loose clothing
- Frequently looking around instead of at merchandise
- Handling items but no eye contact with product
- Visiting fitting rooms multiple times
- Lingering near exits
- Group distractions (one person keeps staff busy while another steals)

**What to Do:**
- Increase presence near customer
- Offer assistance repeatedly
- Radio manager discreetly: "Customer needs assistance in Section 4" (code for suspicious activity)
- NEVER accuse customer of theft
- NEVER physically confront or chase
- NEVER block exits

**If You Witness Theft:**
1. Note detailed description: height, weight, clothing, distinguishing features
2. Note what items were taken
3. Note direction of exit
4. Inform manager immediately
5. Complete Incident Report Form #IR-700
6. Review security footage with manager

### Internal Theft Prevention
- All employee bags checked before leaving (manager spot checks)
- No personal items on sales floor
- Employees must use designated entrance/exit
- No employees ringing up their own purchases
- Random till audits by management
- Access to safe limited to managers only

## 7. Customer Service Excellence

### Service Standards

**The RetailCo Way:**
1. **Acknowledge** - Greet within 30 seconds
2. **Engage** - Ask open-ended questions about their needs
3. **Assist** - Provide product knowledge and recommendations
4. **Close** - Thank them and invite them back

**Product Knowledge Expectations:**
- Know top 20 items in your department (features, benefits, price points)
- Understand current promotions and how they work
- Be able to locate items in other departments (know store layout)
- If you don't know: "Great question! Let me find out for you."

### Handling Difficult Situations

**Upset Customer De-escalation:**
1. **Listen actively** - Let them explain without interrupting
2. **Empathize** - "I understand your frustration..."
3. **Apologize** - Even if not our fault: "I'm sorry you're experiencing this."
4. **Solve** - Offer solutions, give options
5. **Escalate if needed** - "Let me get my manager to help resolve this."

**What NOT to Say:**
- "That's not my department"
- "I don't know"
- "That's our policy" (without explaining why)
- "You should have..."
- "There's nothing I can do"

**What TO Say:**
- "Let me help you with that"
- "Let me find out for you"
- "I can offer you these options..."
- "Here's what I can do for you..."

**Complaint Handling:**
- Thank customer for feedback
- Complete Customer Feedback Form #CF-300
- Offer immediate resolution if possible
- If no immediate fix: Get customer's contact info and follow up within 24 hours
- Manager calls customer within same business day

### Phone Protocol

**Answering Standards:**
- Answer within 3 rings
- "Thank you for calling RetailCo [Location]. This is [Your Name]. How can I help you?"
- Smile while talking (customer can hear it!)
- Never put customer on hold for >2 minutes
- If longer wait: Offer to call them back

**Transferring Calls:**
- Ask permission: "May I transfer you to our [Department]?"
- Give customer the direct extension in case call drops
- Brief the receiving person before transferring
- Don't transfer more than once (get manager help if complex)
